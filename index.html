Executive Summary

Why Reps Know What To Do — But Don’t Do It When It Counts

The biggest outbound performance leak isn’t scripting — it’s emotional inhibition. When stress rises, reps default to avoidance: fewer dials, softer language, and weaker closes. Fix state control, and skill becomes available again.

Map This To Your Team

1) The Pattern

Dial volume drops first — it’s the highest-friction behavior.

When emotional load rises, the body searches for relief. The easiest relief is delay. Delay kills outbound. Leaders call it effort. The nervous system experiences it as threat.

2) The Close Outcome

“I’ll think it over” becomes the dominant outcome.

Buyers avoid discomfort too — especially when a rep’s emotional state triggers distrust. If the rep can’t hold tension and guide emotion, the buyer exits with a delay phrase. Reps then mirror avoidance in follow-up.

The Inconsistency Crisis Starts as a Feeling

You don’t see the problem on dashboards at first. You feel it. The floor gets quieter. The pace drops. A rep opens the dialer… then checks one more tab. Five seconds becomes fifteen. It looks like time management. It isn’t.

Inconsistency is often emotional inhibition. Under pressure, the body tries to reduce discomfort. Delay becomes the micro-escape. Dialing becomes optional. Closing becomes timid.
